Art History Class

All the below artwork I drew for my Art History class in high school.

Ancient Greece: Athena riding a griffon

Impressionist: A blue flower inside of a blue vase resting atop a wooden table. I don't usually draw like this, so I'm proud it turned out well.

Cubism: A shrimp. I drew this cute shrimp when I was excited about having shrimp for dinner.

Black History month: A silhouette of a peacock inspired by Kara Walker's art.

Abstract Expressionism. I like this one! It doesn't represent anything in particular, but I think I did a good job making it look cool.

Pop Art: My cat fursona.

Art Deco: A golden jellyfish surrounded by triangles. When I researched Art Deco, I found that both simple geometric shapes and detailed illustrations in gold fit under the category, so I tried to invoke both.

Tattoo: A stylized woodpecker in front of a skin texture. I was inspired by the beautiful woodpecker I saw beside a stump that morning.

Animation: A gif of my fursona sleeping in a forest. The assignment was to draw a still image reminiscent of 2d animation, but the video lessons on animation that the teacher showed us inspired me to make a little animation of my own.
